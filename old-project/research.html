Research Section

The Research Track emerges as a crucial gateway for students to push their creativity and curiosity towards exploring the biological environment around them. Our 8-weeks research program offers comprehensive guidance, empowering students to craft complete research or review papers from scratch.

Research Courses

Bioinformatics Analysis of Genetic Mutations Associated with Sleep Disorders and Stress in High School Students.

Mazen Abdelsttar Bioinformatics- Mutation Genetics Kevin Atef

This research involved a survey conducted among 150 participants from around the world to investigate the relationship between sleep disorders, stress, and genetic mutations in young adults, highlighting their impact on health and potential for further study.

From Mind to Machine: A Comprehensive Review of Brain-Computer Interfaces.

Omar Elsayed Neuroscience Kevin Atef

This paper explores Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCIs), covering their history, applications, signal acquisition methods, and challenges. It highlights BCIs' transformative impact on assistive technology, medicine, and virtual reality, and suggests solutions to improve usability and functionality.

How Insomnia Stimulates Inflammation in Rheumatoid Arthritis, and how the treatment by neutralization can affect the bones by the utilization of ACPA as a biomarker.

Mazen Rafae Immunology and Molecular Biology Mohamed Hassan

This research paper delves into the impact of Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA) on joints and bones, discussing factors like insomnia and stress. It suggests neutralization treatment using biomarkers like ACPA, potentially revolutionizing RA management through genetic engineering.

Detrimental Effects and Safety Considerations of Formaldehyde Treatment for Hyperhidrosis: A Comparative Review.

Ahmed Zayed Clinical Dermatology Mohamed Hassan

This review examines formaldehyde's effectiveness and health risks in treating hyperhidrosis, comparing it with other treatments. Despite reducing sweat, formaldehyde's safety concerns necessitate careful consideration and exploration of safer alternatives.

Ibrahim Khalil Genetics and Cancer Biology Mina Ashraf

About Us.

We aim to immerse young students in a dynamic environment with biology enthusiasts, motivated mentors, and abundant resources to spark their creativity and curiosity. This program offers opportunities to explore biology beyond the classroom, from the DNA of bacteria to whale anatomy. By raising awareness of biology's importance, students can hone their knowledge, conduct impactful research, and potentially contribute new information to the field.
